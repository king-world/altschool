Nigerian Jollof Rice Recipe

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a vibrant, smoky, and flavorful one-pot dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and spices. It’s a staple in West African celebrations and gatherings, often paired with fried plantains, chicken, or fish. This recipe captures the classic Nigerian version, known for its rich taste and signature “party rice” aroma.


Photo Credit: allrecipes.com

Recipe Information

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cook time: 40 minutes
  • Servings: 6 people
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 4 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 4 red bell peppers
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken or beef stock
  • 2 seasoning cubes (Maggi/Knorr)
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black or white pepper
  • Salt, to taste

Note: tbsp = tablespoon

Instructions

  1. Blend red bell peppers, tomatoes, scotch bonnet, garlic, and ginger into a smooth paste
  2. Heat oil in a large pot, add sliced onions, and sauté for 2 minutes
  3. Stir in tomato paste and fry for 3 minutes to reduce sourness
  4. Add the blended mixture and cook on medium heat until the sauce thickens and oil begins to separate (about 15 minutes)
  5. Season with thyme, curry powder, seasoning cubes, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Stir well
  6. Pour in chicken/beef stock and bring to a simmer
  7. Add washed rice into the sauce, stir, then cover the pot with foil and a tight lid
  8. Reduce heat to low and cook for 25–30 minutes until rice is tender
  9. Fluff rice with a fork, adjust seasoning if needed, and serve hot with plantains, chicken, or salad
